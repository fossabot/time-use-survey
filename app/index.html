Time use survey

DATA

Time use surveys (TUS) measure the amount of time people spend doing various activities, such as paid work, household and family care, personal care, voluntary work, social life, travel, and leisure activities.

The American Time Use Survey (ATUS) measures the amount of time people spend doing various activities, such as paid work, childcare, volunteering, and socializing.

Goal

Visualize duration of activities throughout the day in Europe and America.
Compare people's habits in different countries in Europe and states in America.
Explore how do people spend their time doing various activities based on gender, day of the week, time in Europe and America.

Observations

Differences travel time (transports) between countries in Europe from 2000 to 2010.
Gap household time between gender in Europe.
Happiness the less you work - the happier you are.

Experimental visualization (EUROPE)

Evolution between 2000 and 2010 of two pre-selected activities. And correlation with socio-economic data.

Experimental visualization (EUROPE)

Map and Sunburst (EUROPE)

The map shows the time used for a given activity for each country.

Map and Graph (AMERICA)

The map shows the time used for a given activity for all states or a selected state.
